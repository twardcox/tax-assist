# Statement of Work: UTBIS — Universal Tax Benefit Intelligence System

**Version:** 1.0 (2026-07-10) · **Author:** Travis Cox (PM/PO) with Claude Code · **Status:** DRAFT — pending SOW+ quality gate

## Executive Summary

UTBIS is an AI-assisted tax intelligence platform that compares a taxpayer's financial facts
against a curated library of legal tax benefits (federal, state, county) and reports what is
available now, what is *almost* available, what changes would unlock it, and what a CPA should
review. It is explicitly **not** a filing tool and never recommends sham positions; its output is
a decision aid that tells the user when a CPA conversation becomes worth its fee.

The platform is functionally mature: a TypeScript/Fastify backend with a React frontend, a
63-record benefit library (51 federal + 6 state + 6 county) plus 3 multi-rule strategy stacks, a
59-rule scanner, scenario modeling, CPA packet generation, a 17-source tax-law updater, and a
full Form 1040 + 8-schedule tax calculator with verified IRS PDF fill. All of it has, until now,
run only against synthetic seed data.

This SOW covers the next delivery arc: **making UTBIS produce real value for its first real
user** — the owner's single-member LLC — by entering real facts, adding threshold-trigger
benefits (§41 R&D credit, S-corp election) with "almost available" distance-to-threshold
reporting, and establishing a ≤15-minute monthly books ritual. This is the dogfooding milestone
that converts UTBIS from a demo into a working tool, and it is the foundation for subsequent
library growth and an eventual merge to `main`.

## Business Objectives

1. **First real user (dogfooding):** the owner's LLC profile, entered through the app, produces a
   real scan result with at least the two standing tax triggers tracked as live thresholds —
   measurable as a scan report showing §41 and S-corp with distance-to-threshold against real books.
2. **Trigger-style "almost available" reporting:** the scanner reports below-threshold benefits
   with the dollar distance to qualification (per the lab's standing triggers: ~$5k/yr cash spend →
   §41 CPA conversation; ~$40k/yr net profit → S-corp election) — measurable as a report section
   mirroring a trigger table (threshold, current value, fired/not-fired).
3. **Sustainable bookkeeping loop:** a documented monthly books ritual (CSV append → aggregates →
   scan → review → backup). The ≤15-minute duration is an **aspirational target** (PM/PO
   2026-07-10), not an acceptance gate.
4. **Privacy holds in a public repo:** no real financial fact is ever committable — measurable by
   the `git check-ignore` guard test staying green and real data living only in the local
   PostgreSQL database.

## Scope

### In Scope

- Owner's real facts entered via the My Data UI (per design review 2026-07-10: DB, not YAML overlay)
- §41 R&D credit benefit record (incl. §174A immediate-expensing note, QSB payroll-tax offset path)
- S-corp election benefit extended with the ~$40k net-profit breakeven heuristic
- Threshold-trigger ("almost available" with distance-to-threshold) evaluation and report section
- Monthly books ritual: CSV validation, aggregate recompute, scan trigger (Story C — depends on the above)
- Local PostgreSQL backup (in scope per PM/PO 2026-07-10): a `pg_dump`-based backup step, folded into the monthly ritual, with one verified restore
- Synthetic fixtures only in committed tests; real values never leave the local DB / `user_data/private/`

### Out of Scope

- Computing the §41 credit amount or preparing elections/filings (UTBIS flags CPA conversations only)
- Multi-tenant/hosted SaaS deployment (local, single-machine operation stays the model)
- Phase C cross-section data-redundancy reconciliation (separately flagged effort touching the tax calculator)
- Any integration coupling to the Public Data Discovery Lab's management ledger (quarterly reconciliation is a human ritual by standing decision)
- Broader benefit-library expansion beyond the two trigger benefits (next arc, after this milestone)

## Constraints

- **Budget:** founder-hours are the operative currency (lab cost-discipline §1.8; labor notionally booked at $150/hr in the management ledger). LLM/API spend tracked per invoice; currently trivial.
- **Timeline:** no external deadline; paced by founder availability. Tax-year 2026 relevance means the trigger reporting is most useful before Q4 2026 planning.
- **Resources:** solo founder (PM/PO + developer + first user), AI-augmented via Claude Code.
- **Technical:** public GitHub repo — real financial data must never be committable; existing stack is fixed (TypeScript/Fastify/PostgreSQL/React, see Architecture doc); CSV books in `user_data/private/books/` remain the cash system of record regardless of app features.

## Assumptions

- A1: The seeded/entered owner profile in the local DB is accurate enough to drive real scan results (owner keeps TODO fields current via the worksheet).
- A2: The ~$5k (§41) and ~$40k (S-corp) thresholds are heuristics for "start the CPA conversation," not tax advice; precision beyond that is out of scope.
- A3: The existing scanner rule engine and benefit-record schema can express threshold-distance semantics without a rewrite (validated during design; revisit if false).
- A4: Single-machine local operation; no new infrastructure.
